Project Name: Soul Companion

Overview

The project “Soul Companion”, a game that focuses on the puzzle and exploration concept set in a world after an apocalypse that involves interactions between the user and the player.

Motivation

As a group, we all enjoy playing games one way or another and that this could be a learning experience as to how game developers make games interesting to the eyes of the players. As an IT perspective this can lead to working as a team where one’s individual idea could sparks other people’s interests or passions.

Description

The project “Soul Companion” is a game that will be made using GameMaker and would be operated on Windows OS as a .exe file.

The main concept or objective of the game is to control the player to “Create Interaction and Explore”. Interaction can mean anything from being influence by your environment or surroundings and likewise, taking the action to influence others around you. Explore in my words can be taken as two ways, one is travelling, and the other is learning new ways to adapt or complete an objective in a new possible way.

The user will be thrown into a mysterious world after an unknown entity has cause the world’s demise, however this world is rapidly evolving beyond what its original natural habitat or environment used to be capable of. You start off with controlling a silent player that would later start to interact with its environment and surroundings.

The user will be given no plot directions outside of just instructions on how to play the game. The game will simply, or more specifically the player will tell you that they will unconsciously want to interact more with you and the objects or people around them. If there is a main focus, then it would be that you, the user must help the player survive.

There will be action or special commands that the user can ask the player to do or respond, such as interacting with objects or people.

Action commands can be like interact, accept, decline, and will depend on the situation you are in the game.

An example of a special command is that you can ask the player how they are feeling right now, or to dance to the background music that only you can hear.

In terms of background music or audio, I am hoping we can create something that lean towards ambience music with a mix of fantasy or another genre while also reminding the user (you) or the player that this is a post-apocalyptic world.

As this is a game that is set in a post-apocalyptic world that changes it’s environment, every area’s design will be different to its predecessor and will contribute to the game having a more exploration theme. An example can be an abandoned city where it is still running due to being affected by its changing environment.

In this game, there is no such thing as game overs. So, when you come across a difficult situation, the player will prompt the user to just retry into they complete it. However, there will be situations where your choices are permanent, and you will never be able to go back again unless you start from the very beginning. These choices will not really cause a grave error on your end and will not affect your overall experience with the player.

While the target audience is for anybody who enjoy games in general, it is aimed towards those who enjoy solving puzzles and wanting to explore new areas while creating your own story that would test the bonds between the user and the player. You will also enjoy this game if you like to interact with the player and help them solve their problem.

I like to mention that there is actually no story given but more like you imagine your own story which is the journey you have with the player as you travel from place to place.

    - Commitment to understanding and using new technology

    These can all be achievable by simply just having a strong passion to make or play games while also being innovative.

    Outcome

    If this game is completed, or a prototype version featuring the interactions made by you (The user) and the player throughout the game, the end results would show that you have a connection with the player, no matter how it turns out. Because this is a game, the users would normally follow instructions on how to play the game and progress through, however my intentions are that I want the users to find out by themselves by connecting with the player that they are controlling, and that they will share a bond that only they would know of. The impact of this development is that the user would empathise with the player by helping them, and in return will make the user feel satisfied.
